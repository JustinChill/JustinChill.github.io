Anscombe’s quartet

Frank Anscombe was an English statistician who stressed the importance of computers being able to perform calculations and graph data visually. To illustrate his point he devised four data sets that appear nearly identical in tabular form, but have very different distributions and appear very different when graphed.

Anscombe's quartet is often used to illustrate the importance of looking at a set of data graphically before starting to analyze according to a particular type of relationship, and the inadequacy of basic statistic properties for describing realistic datasets.
