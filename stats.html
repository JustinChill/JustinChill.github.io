How does geolocation work?

May 2021

Ever wonder how Google knows where you are searching from? The answer is geolocation: finding the latitude and longitude coordinates of a particular location. When a device uses the internet, it broadcasts a unique identifier called an IP address. Internet service providers (and the infrastructure handling your query) use this number to process your request, and ISPs are allocated specific ranges of IP addresses. The way IP-based geolocation works is simply by checking which IP range is used by which area, using an IP-to-location database. So, you can find the information like the country and the city that your users come from, just by knowing their IP address. It's not terribly precise, and it is susceptible to spoofing, but it's a good data point.
