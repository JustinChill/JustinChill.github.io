Metafy

Great Gaming via Great Training

Thanks to a global pandemic, the videogame industry has seen a surge in demand, with global gaming sales rising to nearly $180 billion in 2020. Metafy is building a marketplace to match gamers with instructors, allowing average players to learn from expert coaches. By focusing on how to make great players become great teachers, both coaches and students win.

Metafy has grown quickly

Metafy caters to aspiring professionals as well as casual players wanting an advantage, parents looking to keep up with their kids, and curious gamers looking to learn quickly. Metafy has raised a total of $8.7M in funding over 2 rounds: $3.2M in December 2020 and $5.5M in May 2021.

Growing in public

Software is constantly growing and evolving. Building in public allows startups to take their audience & community along for the ride, sharing the good and the bad of the journey of building a business. This 5,000 word letter to investors from CEO John Fabian is a great example of what this looks like in practice. Here's an excerpt:

We had 36K visitors throughout July, and of that, 31K were brand new users. Our session duration is up 10% MOM at 1m35s so these new users are navigating the product and are quick to understand how to navigate our games and coaches.

Metafy Support

Metafy uses Discord and Intercom to offer support to their growing audience. It's a great way to engage with a rabid fan base, and as the user feedback above shows it's clearly working. I see a few challenges in the near term for the support team:

  • Organizing support. Browsing through the Discord #support channel I can see (timely!) replies from the whole team. This is great (real time feedback, clear customer-centric mindset from execs) but expensive. We'll need to put in processes to organize and streamline work for the support team.
  • Maintaining a brand identity. Personality is everything, and as the user base and company grows, maintaining a quirky and unique brand personality will be a challenge. We'll need to make sure that our front line support agents are able to bring their full selves to work and continue to engage users in a personal way.
