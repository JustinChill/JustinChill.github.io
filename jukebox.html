Home modification can be as simple as installing a new doorknob that's easy to use, adding a grab bar in the right place or putting in a ramp to get in through the back door. It may even involve widening a door or installing a special sink or elevator. Here are some design and product suggestions from AARP's excellent "How to Develop A Home Modification Initiative" guide.

Example home modifications:

  • Zero-threshold doorways
  • Bright lighting by entries
  • Handrails on both sides of steps
  • Easy-glide drawers
  • Rocker-style light switches
  • Lever handle, anti-scald faucets on sink, bathtub, and shower

Additional resources

  • A local Area Agency on Aging (AAA) is a public or private nonprofit agency designated by a state to address the needs and concerns of all older persons at the regional and local levels. AAAs have great programs set up to pay for things such as meals, home help, transportation, and home modifications for people with disabilities or those who are aging
  • AEMA, the Association of Members of the Accessibility Industry, is a trade group of companies that make elevators and lifts, stairway chairlifts and similar products.
  • The National Resource Center on Supportive Housing and Home Modification, based at the University of Southern California, promotes aging at home for the elderly and persons aging with a disability.
  • The National Association of Home Builders. NAHB is a federation of more than 700 state and local home builder associations (HBAs). This organization oversees the CAPS designation.
