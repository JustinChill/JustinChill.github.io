Penrose Triangle

The Penrose triangle is a triangular impossible object. It was first created by the Swedish artist Oscar Reutersvärd in 1934. The psychiatrist Lionel Penrose and his mathematician son Roger Penrose independently devised and popularized it in the 1950s, describing it as "impossibility in its purest form". It is featured prominently in the works of artist M. C. Escher, whose earlier depictions of impossible objects partly inspired it.

There are two steps to recreating the Penrose triangle using CSS. First draw 3 squares, stacked on top of each other. Rotate the second square 120 degrees and the third square 240 degrees. Cut-off the space where the squares don't overlap.

Café wall ☕️
